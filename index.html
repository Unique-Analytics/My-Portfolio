Data Discovery


Designated Marketing Area

(DMA) is a region where the population can receive the same (or similar) television and radio station offerings,and can also include other types of media including news and Internet content. We use DMA strategically as an integral part of our analytic solutions. Most marketing firms and social media networks such as Facebook use DMA's in order to geo-target their content to the customers who have similar interests and propensities.

Cultural

Culture turns a person into a people, turning the perception of 'I' into 'We'. Different cultural groups think, feel, and act differently. A culture is a way of life of a group of people that are passed along by communication and imitation from one generation to the next. Analyzing the culture of your target audience delivers your business strategy.

Demographics

I use the 4 quadrants methodology in our analysis of Demographics. This essentially says that the more demographics your product or service reaches, the more successful your campaign will be. My goal is to ensure that your product or service accomplishes this task.

Sociographic

I take the target market down to the level of the individual, where I determine the specific values, attitudes, friends, hobbies, passions, and influences. Essentially, it allows me to discover what really pushes your customers buttons.

Geographics

I fully embrace that home is where your heart is. Using Designated Market areas we discover how a customers environment inflences their buying behavior.

Sentiment Analysis

I take the sentiment of customers seriously, this is why I have developed our own in-house tool that measures the customers sentiment towards a similar brands or product. I don't just tell you they like or dislike a product. I also tell you specifically what they like or dislike about a product/service.
